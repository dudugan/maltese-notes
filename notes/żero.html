What Maltese is Like

Maltese is a Semitic language. That means it is more closely related to Arabic and Hebrew than it is to Italian or any other language. This is great news for me, but probably less great news for you.

However, more than 50% of the Maltese vocabulary derives from Italian, and only around 30% derives from Arabic. Assuming you know English or a Romance language and not a Semitic language, the hard part will be the grammar, not the vocabulary.

Fortunately, Maltese grammar is actually very simple! At least compared to Arabic and Hebrew. There are not many verb conjugations, and they are easy to memorize. Here are what I predict will be the hard parts (unless you speak Arabic):

  • A lot of the time, the plural form of a noun will not be predictable from the singular form. Think mouse and mice.
  • If you don't speak a language with grammatical gender, remembering and using gender in Maltese will be tough.
  • The syntax (word order) of Maltese is often different from English.
