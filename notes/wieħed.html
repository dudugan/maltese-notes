Pronunciation and Writing

(Note that most of my example words are from Wikipedia)

Maltese uses a phonetic romanization. In other words, the pronunciation of a word can always be deduced from how it is written, and the language is written using the Latin alphabet.

Maltese has X consonants and X vowels which might be difficult for English speakers, either to read or pronounce:

letter IPA* example sound
ċ tʃ ċavetta
‘key’		 chill
ġ dʒ ġar
‘neighbor’		 budge
g g gidba always
o ɔ ħobż English “o” in “hot”
u u ħelu English “oo” in “food”

Here are a few more pronunciation tips:

  • Doubled letters like in ċavetta are pronounced twice as long.
  • The letters “b”, “d”, “g”, and “ġ” sound like “p”, “t”, “k”, and “ċ” at the ends of words.
  • Maltese doesn't use undotted “c”, so if you see it, it's just shorthand for “ċ”.

Honestly, I think the best way to learn the pronunciation of a language is just to listen to tons of videos in that language. So don't worry too much for now.

Every other letter is pronounced basically the same as in Spanish or Italian or Latin.
